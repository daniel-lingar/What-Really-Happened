# Capability Statement

## What Really Happened (WRH) – Trauma Education & Recovery Pilot System

---

## Core Competencies

**What Really Happened (WRH)** delivers a structured, facilitator-led trauma education and recovery system designed for individuals experiencing Complex PTSD (C-PTSD).

Key capabilities include:

- Structured, session-based trauma education curriculum (30-session model)
- Facilitator-guided group implementation framework
- Participant safety systems (decompression protocols, Plan B stabilization tools)
- Behavioral pattern recognition and emotional regulation training
- Integrated software portal for program navigation and operational support
- Evaluation-ready pilot framework with pre/post measurement tools

The WRH model translates complex trauma theory into **repeatable, scalable, and facilitator-driven interventions** suitable for institutional environments.

---

## Differentiators

WRH is designed specifically for **institutional deployment**, not informal self-help delivery.

- **Boundary-Based Architecture**  
  Separation of clinical content, legal/compliance systems, and software delivery ensures audit clarity and operational control.

- **Facilitator-First Model**  
  No reliance on unsupervised digital therapy. All interventions are guided, structured, and protocol-driven.

- **Operationally Deployable**  
  Includes readiness checklists, facilitator training pathways, and implementation controls for pilot environments.

- **AI-Constrained Support Tools**  
  Software components are restricted to navigation and structured reflection—no diagnostic or therapeutic automation.

- **Evaluation-Ready Design**  
  Built-in outcome tracking framework aligned with pilot measurement and reporting requirements.

## Compliance & Risk Posture

- No storage of sensitive participant data within public repositories
- Role-based access model for facilitators, administrators, and developers

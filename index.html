Capability Overview

What Really Happened (WRH) is a 75-session non-clinical psychoeducational curriculum developed by Capitol Contracts LLC. It teaches trauma responses, addiction patterns, freeze responses, shame cycles, executive-function disruption, avoidance, collapse, and survival behavior as predictable mechanisms rather than character flaws.

The curriculum does not ask participants to relive, disclose, or process trauma in a therapy-style format. It teaches observable patterns, mechanism language, interruption points, facilitator scripts, and safer replacement tools.

Core Purpose

People cannot interrupt a pattern they have never been taught to see.

WRH translates trauma, addiction, freeze responses, shame cycles, executive-function disruption, avoidance, collapse, and survival behavior into plain-language system logic.

Session Coverage

Sessions 1–26

System logic, survival-based neural adaptation, attachment disruption, grief, fawn, hypervigilance, freeze, dissociation, shame, boundaries, burnout, regulation, recovery trajectory, co-regulation, growth, and identity reconstruction.

Sessions 27–56

Behavioral initialization, architecture mapping, reinforcement patterns, baseline regulation, identity conditioning, avoidance, recursive loops, somatic encoding, triggers, escalation, fight/flight/freeze, decision inhibition, accountability, and stabilization.

Sessions 57–75

Executive function under load, institutional stress, stigma, justice-system misclassification, veteran navigation, first responder exposure, workplace stress, healthcare continuity, family dynamics, advocacy access, autonomy restoration, resilience, and integrated systems modeling.

Operational Safety & Standardization

No-Disclosure Design

WRH teaches patterns without requiring participants to expose personal trauma history or process trauma in group.

Facilitator Standardization

Part IV supports consistent delivery through facilitator rules, field cards, safety language, protocols, checklists, reporting, and certification path materials.

Crisis Response Boundary

Session 76 and the Universal Crisis Package are operational safety resources, not a replacement for licensed crisis care or agency escalation policy.

Master Curriculum Architecture

The WRH Master Curriculum is organized into three specialized parts and a facilitator toolkit, totaling 76 operational sessions.

Part I: The System Logic Map (Sessions 1–26)

Focus: Foundational survival laws, pattern recognition, and nervous-system adaptation. Teaches the "Laws of Survival" as physiological wiring rather than character flaws.

Part II: The Core Operational Journey (Sessions 27–56)

Focus: Mechanical loops, wiring patterns, and biological programs. Provides practical regulation tools and behavioral interruption strategies for pilot environments.

Part III: Advanced System Specialization (Sessions 57–75)

Focus: Institutional misreadings, relapse/collapse logic, and long-term accountability. Analyzes cross-system integration and multi-system engagement friction.

Part IV: Facilitator Standardization Toolkit

Includes: Session 76 (Crisis Response), Master Glossary, Facilitator Scripts, Participant Workbooks, and the Universal Crisis Package.

Core Philosophy

"People cannot interrupt a pattern they have never been taught to see."

WRH reframes survival responses as predictable patterns rather than character defects. By teaching the "machine code" of trauma, we empower participants to see the wiring and interrupt the loops without requiring personal disclosure.
